11.3 Capacitance

11.3.1 Capacitance

Current can flow through wires when there is a complete ciruit

If you apply an emf to an open circuit what happens?

Some current will flow until the electric field due to the charge separation is equal to the applied E field

How much charge will flow depend on the geometry

The ratio of Charge that can flow / applied Voltage is the Capacitance

C = Q / V

With a high Capacitance we can store a more Charge for the same voltage

The units of capacitance are Coulombs per Volt CV-1 or Farads (F)

11.3.3 Capacitors in series and parallel

Series

If you put two Capacitors in parallel, what happens to the voltage? What else changes?

The voltage stays the same, the charge that can be held is added.

As C=Q/V and QT/VT = Q1/V1 + Q2/V2

The Capacitance of two capacitors in parallel is CT = C1 + C2

Parallel

If you put two Capacitors in series, what happens to the voltage? What else changes?

The voltage is split, the charge that flows is equal (series).

As 1/C=Q/V and VT/QT = V1/Q1 + V2/Q2

The Capacitance of two capacitors in parallel is 1/CT = 1/C1 + 1/C2

11.3.4 Resistor-capacitor (RC) series circuits

A capacitor charges when the voltage across it is greater than Q/C (from C=Q/V).

ΔV = Applied voltage - Q/C

The ΔV causes a current to flow (depending on the resistance of the circuit)

A resistor reduces the current that flows, extending the time taken to charge
